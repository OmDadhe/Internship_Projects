About Dr. Kalam

Dr. Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam (15 October 1931 – 27 July 2015) was an Indian aerospace scientist and statesman who served as the 11th President of India from 2002 to 2007. Known as the "Missile Man of India," he played a pivotal role in the development of India's missile and nuclear weapons programs. A humble visionary, he inspired millions with his dedication to science, education, and youth empowerment.

Throughout his life, Dr. Kalam remained committed to transforming India into a developed nation and was beloved for his simple lifestyle and accessible approach to leadership. His vision for India, articulated in his writings and speeches, continues to inspire generations.

Early Life

Born in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, into a modest family, Kalam overcame financial hardships by selling newspapers as a child. His curiosity for science led him to study aerospace engineering at the Madras Institute of Technology, laying the foundation for his remarkable career.

Growing up in a diverse environment, young Kalam was deeply influenced by his family values, particularly from his father who taught him the importance of integrity and self-discipline. Despite financial constraints, his parents ensured he received education, recognizing his intellectual potential from an early age.

Life Timeline

1931

Born in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu on October 15.

1955

Graduated in Physics from Saint Joseph's College, Tiruchirappalli.

1960

Joined DRDO as a scientist after graduating from Madras Institute of Technology.

1969

Transferred to ISRO where he was project director of India's first satellite launch vehicle.

1980

Successfully deployed Rohini satellite near earth's orbit with SLV-III.

1990s

Served as the Chief Scientific Adviser to the Prime Minister.

1998

Led India's Pokhran-II nuclear tests which established India as a nuclear power.

2002

Elected as the 11th President of India with an overwhelming majority.

2007

Completed presidential term and returned to civilian life, focusing on teaching.

2015

Passed away on July 27 while delivering a lecture at IIM Shillong.

Books by Dr. Kalam

Wings of Fire

Published: 1999

An autobiography that chronicles his inspiring journey from humble beginnings to a celebrated scientist. The book has been translated into multiple languages and remains one of the most influential autobiographies in modern India.

Ignited Minds

Published: 2002

A book aimed at inspiring the youth of India to dream big and contribute to the nation. Dr. Kalam shares his interactions with students across the country and presents his vision for a developed India.

India 2020

Published: 1998

This visionary book outlines Dr. Kalam's roadmap for transforming India into a developed nation by 2020. He identifies five areas where India needs to build core competencies: agriculture, education, healthcare, information technology, and infrastructure.

Turning Points

Published: 2012

A sequel to "Wings of Fire," this book focuses on Dr. Kalam's presidential years. He shares his experiences at the highest office and the decisions that shaped modern India during his tenure.
